Sample Problem

Here's an example of the type of engaging problem our Stage 1 students work on:

Pattern Detective

Emma notices that when she arranges her toy blocks in a square pattern, she always has some blocks left over. When she makes a 3×3 square, she has 4 blocks left. When she makes a 4×4 square, she has 4 blocks left. When she makes a 5×5 square, she has 4 blocks left.

How many blocks does Emma have in total?

Teaching Approach:

This problem introduces students to modular arithmetic concepts in an age-appropriate way. Students learn to look for patterns, make connections between different cases, and develop systematic thinking. The problem encourages multiple solution strategies and helps students understand that mathematics is about finding patterns and relationships.
