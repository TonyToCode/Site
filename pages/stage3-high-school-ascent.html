Here's an example of the challenging problems our Stage 3 students master:

Complex Numbers and Geometry

Let z₁, z₂, z₃ be complex numbers such that |z₁| = |z₂| = |z₃| = 1 and z₁ + z₂ + z₃ = 0. If z₁z₂ + z₂z₃ + z₃z₁ = 0, find |z₁ - z₂|².

Answer choices: (A) 1 (B) 2 (C) 3 (D) 4 (E) 6

Solution Strategy:

Since z₁, z₂, z₃ lie on the unit circle and sum to zero, they form vertices of a triangle centered at the origin.

Using the constraint z₁z₂ + z₂z₃ + z₃z₁ = 0 and properties of complex numbers on the unit circle.

This leads to the conclusion that the triangle is equilateral, giving |z₁ - z₂|² = 3.
